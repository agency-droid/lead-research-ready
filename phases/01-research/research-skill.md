# Phase 1 — Research Skill

> **Goal:** In 10-15 minutes, gather enough information about a prospect to build a personalized landing page. Output is a filled-out Research Brief that feeds directly into Phase 2.

---

## What You Need Before Starting

- Prospect's name (first name at minimum)
- At least ONE of: website URL, Instagram handle, LinkedIn URL

Optional but valuable:
- Screenshots of their Instagram grid or website
- Any content they've posted (videos, posts, offers)
- Their industry / niche
- Their target audience

---

## Step-by-Step

### Step 1 — Collect All Links

Ask (or check Camille's notes) for:
- [ ] Website URL
- [ ] Instagram URL or handle
- [ ] LinkedIn URL
- [ ] Any other relevant links (Linktree, YouTube, etc.)

### Step 2 — Research Their Business

Visit each link and extract:

**From website:**
- What do they do / what service do they offer?
- Who is their audience?
- What's their main CTA (call to action)?
- What's missing or weak on their current site?
- Any testimonials or social proof worth highlighting?

**From Instagram:**
- What content do they post? (Behind the scenes, education, promos, lifestyle?)
- What's their tone? (Professional, casual, inspirational?)
- Do they have a strong personal brand or is it inconsistent?
- Any signature phrases, hashtags, or recurring themes?

**From LinkedIn:**
- Title / credentials
- Years of experience
- Any standout achievements or awards

### Step 3 — Fill Out the Research Brief

Copy the template from `../../templates/research-brief-template.md` and fill it in for this prospect.

Save the filled brief as:
```
phases/01-research/briefs/[prospect-name]-brief.md
```

Example: `phases/01-research/briefs/angie-brief.md`

### Step 4 — Hand Off to Phase 2

Once the brief is complete:
1. Open `../../templates/site-prompt-template.md`
2. Fill the `[RESEARCH BRIEF]` section with the brief you just created
3. That completed prompt is what goes into Lovable to build the site

---

## Research Tips

- **Don't overthink it.** You need enough to make the site feel personal — not a full audit.
- **Look for their "hero moment."** What's the one thing they're known for or most proud of? Lead with that on the site.
- **Spot the gap.** What does their current presence NOT do well? The Pocket Profile should visibly do that better.
- **Tone match.** A fitness coach's site should feel different from a financial advisor's. Match their energy.

---

## Output Checklist

Before handing off to Phase 2, confirm:

- [ ] Prospect name + title / niche filled in
- [ ] At least 1-2 sentences describing what they do and who they serve
- [ ] Their main offer or CTA identified
- [ ] Tone/style noted (professional, energetic, warm, etc.)
- [ ] At least one differentiator or standout fact noted
- [ ] Any specific visuals or branding details noted (colors, logo, imagery style)
